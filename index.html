interviewer: what excites you? what are your hobbies and interests outside music? (other than lego)

Ash: well, on an abstract level I'm interested in self-developing systems; intelligence both artificial and human has been a preoccupation of mine since I was a child. the concept of thought independent of neural-chemical stimuli is central to my work and even that is reliant upon decentralised collatoral structures... to me this is incredibly poetic. currently there is a kind of malaise about it all which is problematic for sure... but that's not exactly what gets me out of bed in the morning. it's exciting to imagine real time translation as well as real time conversation between musically adept humans and machines - the possibilities of musical improv with machines based on their true capabilities are endless. we are at the brink of that and I want to be a part of that push. Another point of interest to me lies within music "automation"; the idea that musicians would conceivably be able to realise their ideas without the level of raw consummate skill they have historically needed. rhythm is both a big one here, but also phrasing. a machine could soon play guitar far better than most humans, which seems like it could actually encourage people to get involved with music and push their own boundaries. a real win for the "machine" side of things too, I mean... you can play guitar and bass and drums etc., but why? what's stopping the computer? this is not a sterile calculation (like are robots better at x than humans?) it's an aesthetic one. next topic-justin timberlake
